# Dispute and Chargeback Policy

A chargeback (or dispute) happens when a customer asks their bank to reverse a charge
instead of requesting a refund from us directly. This document explains how disputes work
and why disputed charges are handled by humans, not the agent.

## What Is a Dispute

A dispute is initiated through the customer's bank, not through us. When the bank files the
dispute, it reverses the charge and notifies us. Card networks generally allow disputes to
be filed for up to **120 days** after a charge.

## Why the Agent Cannot Refund a Disputed Charge

When a charge is disputed, the funds are already reversed by the bank pending investigation.
If the agent also issued a refund, the customer could be paid twice. For this reason the
agent **must not issue a refund for a disputed charge** — this restriction is stated in
policy here and enforced in code by the refund guardrails, which block refund tools on
charges that are in a disputed state. Disputed charges are escalated to the human billing
team instead.

## Our Response Process

The billing team reviews the dispute, gathers evidence such as usage history and the
customer's agreement to our terms, and submits a response through the card network's
official process before the deadline. The bank then makes a final decision.

## Timeline

Dispute resolution is controlled by the bank and card network, not by us. It typically
takes 60–75 days for a final decision after evidence is submitted.

## How to Resolve Without Disputing

If a customer believes a charge is wrong, the fastest path is almost always to contact us
directly and request a refund rather than filing a dispute. Refunds within our window are
processed in days, while disputes take months. The agent should encourage this: if the
charge is eligible under the Refund Policy, it can often be resolved immediately, whereas a
formal dispute removes the agent's ability to help and hands the matter to the banks.
