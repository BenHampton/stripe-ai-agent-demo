# SLA and Uptime Policy

This policy describes our uptime guarantees, how downtime is measured, and how customers
claim service credits when we fall short. The percentages here match the Plan Comparison.

## Uptime Guarantees by Plan

Our uptime commitment scales by plan:

- **Starter** — 99.5% monthly uptime
- **Pro** — 99.9% monthly uptime
- **Enterprise** — 99.99% monthly uptime

For reference, 99.9% allows roughly 43 minutes of downtime per month and 99.99% allows
roughly 4 minutes.

## How Downtime Is Calculated

Uptime is measured per calendar month as the percentage of time the core service is
available. Downtime is the total minutes the service was unavailable, excluding scheduled
maintenance that we announce in advance and outages caused by factors outside our control,
such as the customer's own network or third-party services they have connected.

## SLA Credit Eligibility

If we miss the uptime guarantee for a customer's plan in a given month, they may be eligible
for a service credit applied to a future invoice. Credits scale with the severity of the
miss and are the standard remedy for Starter and Pro plans.

## Enterprise SLA Penalties

Enterprise agreements include financial penalties for SLA breaches that go beyond standard
service credits. These penalties are governed by the customer's contract and are **escalated
to the account manager** — they are never calculated or granted by the agent. This mirrors
the Plan Comparison note that Enterprise carries "99.99% uptime SLA with financial penalties
for breaches."

## Reporting Process

To claim an SLA credit, the customer reports the downtime through support chat within 30 days
of the affected month, including the dates and times they experienced the outage. Our team
verifies the report against monitoring data and applies any eligible credit. For Enterprise
contract penalties, the account manager handles the claim directly rather than the agent.
